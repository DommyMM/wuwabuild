Build card v2 — wire ranks into the profile card.

Three references on the table: WaveMate (compact + ranked + roll-aware), our current wuwa.build card (clean, no rank surface, no per-substat rolls), and akasha.cv's Genshin card (drops the talent tree to make room for a rank module + radar + roll value aggregate). This doc anatomizes each, settles a decision per module, then specs the new card down to columns, paddings, fonts, and pill anatomy.

tldr

Drop the forte node grid. Collapse forte into a 5-pill talent row next to the weapon. Put a rank module in its place — tier letter, board-scope chips, % + #/total, computed damage. Add roll dots to every echo substat. Add a roll-value aggregate bar beneath the 5 echoes summing rolls per substat with a single RV total. Keep splash, sequence ladder, weapon block, stat list, sonata, CV. Don't touch the right-side stat list or the splash treatment — they're the most polished part of the card and reading them tested well already.

01 · the three references

01Anatomy pass.

Read each card as a stack of modules and ask: what is it actually surfacing, and what is the priority order it forces on the eye? The three differ less in styling than in which information they decide is worth screen real estate.

WaveMate 1456 × 884
Steal the rank pill

Information density is the entire pitch. Stat table, weapon, forte tree, rank, sonata row, and 5 echoes all fit in a 16:10 frame. Branded "wavemate.app" watermark + version stamp.

  • Rank module is the headline. A big "S" tier mark, two ranks (board #125 / global #4220), a score (120.43), and a progress bar to next tier. Lives where ours has nothing.
  • Color-coded substat names. Crit substats magenta, energy regen also tinted, neutral/flat values white. Reads the build's quality at a glance without numerics.
  • Per-echo cost shown as a small numbered ring on the echo art (4/3/3/1/1).
  • Forte tree is icon-only, no node grid — 5 columns each showing the talent chain and final Lv. Compact but less canonical than ours.
  • Two-column stat table competes with the weapon and forte for attention; lots of "0%" lines (Basic ATK, Heavy ATK, Healing) take up rows for no information.
  • No roll-count signal. CV per echo only.
wuwa.build (current) 2400 × 1000
Keep the bones

Editorial / atmospheric. Full-bleed splash, breakdowns under base stats (10,300 +5,876), CV pill, sonata pill, all the typographic restraint the design system asks for.

  • Splash is the hero. No other card uses splash this well. Untouchable.
  • Sequence ladder on the left edge of the center column — vertical 6-up rail. Stays.
  • Forte node grid (5×3) is canonical and dense but never the thing anyone shares the card for.
  • Stat list is right-edge anchored with breakdowns. Best part of the card. Stays.
  • No rank surface. The number that decides "is this build good" is missing from the build card.
  • Echo cards are pretty but the four substat rows give equal weight to a 4-roll Crit DMG and a 1-roll DEF flat. No roll signal.
  • No aggregate roll value — there's a single CV at top of each echo and a 208.4 CV total, but no "where did the rolls go" breakdown.
akasha.cv Genshin · ~2160 × 980
Adopt the philosophy

The card exists to surface rank. Akasha treats the build card as a leaderboard certificate, not a screenshot replacement. Notable: they delete the talent tree entirely.

  • Talent levels collapse to 3 small pills on the right edge of the splash (10 NA, 13 Skill, 13 Burst). Crown glyph for max. We can do the same with 5 pills.
  • Top-right is reserved for the rank module: radar chart → team comp row → TOP 0.12% + weapon-type pill → "1828 / 1532212" rank.
  • Roll dots on every substat. "•" through "••••" left of each substat. Single most useful per-piece signal on the card.
  • Aggregate roll bar at the bottom: "×20 ✸ 68.8% · ×12 ✸ 76.2% · …" with priority stats outlined in gold, total "×35 · RV 3030%" on the right. Steal this verbatim.
  • Substat names dimmed when "wasted" (low-roll or off-priority); priority stats stay high-contrast. Earns its information density.
  • Radar chart is great for Genshin where stats are bounded; in wuwa we have a wider stat space and breakdowns already do half this job. Optional — see Q3.

04Down to the pixel.

Each new or changed module specced with tokens you can implement against. All values come from colors_and_type.css. Mocks are at 1× of their actual canvas size where possible.

A · Rank module
new

Sits where the forte node grid used to live. Five elements in priority order: tier letter (visual anchor, the thing you screenshot for), scope chips (which board this rank is on — weapon × sequence × playstyle), TOP percentage (the read-at-a-glance number), numeric rank / total (proof), computed damage (the underlying score, right-edge). Use the active-tab gradient from the design system (gold → highlight → dark) as the surface fill so it reads as "this is the active state of the build".

D · Roll-value aggregate bar
new

Full-width strip beneath the 5 echoes. Sums rolls by substat across the whole build. Priority substats (defined by the build's character/role) are outlined in gold; off-priority are neutral pills. The right edge carries the RV total — the single number that says "this build used N total rolls and they were worth Y%". This is the data backbone for the rank module: same score, more transparent.

05 · open questions

05Decide before we build.

Eight calls to make. My lean for each is highlighted gold — happy to defer to you on any.

Q1 · rank scope chips Which scope does the card show by default?

A build can sit on multiple boards (weapon × sequence × playstyle). The card has room for one tier letter + one TOP %. Pick the user's best rank or the most restrictive?

Best of all eligible boards Most restrictive (S0 + matching weapon) Sticky to last viewed
Q2 · tier letter palette Tier color gradient.

Pure gold-monochrome or full S/A/B/C/D color ladder?

Gold for S, silver for A, bronze for B, neutral C/D All gold (matches "one accent" brief) Element-tinted to character
Q3 · radar chart Adopt akasha's radar or skip?

Radar adds visual interest but our stat list + breakdowns already show distribution. Worry: it competes with the rank module for the "look at this" slot.

Default on, replaces stat list Skip in v2; revisit as a tweak after launch Tweak toggle from day one
Q4 · forte tree on /edit Do we drop the forte tree from /edit too, or only from the card?

Editor still needs full forte input for damage calc. But the render of forte can match the card.

Keep full tree in editor, talent pill row on card only Pill row everywhere
Q5 · priority stat source Where do priority stats come from?

The RV bar tints priority stats gold. We need a per-character priority list.

Curated per-character (matches the leaderboard scope) Inferred from the user's actual rolls User-overridable
Q6 · CV vs RV — both? Two summary scores on the card?

CV is the wuwa community's vernacular; RV is more honest about what rolled where. Showing both is more transparent but doubles the cognitive load.

Both — CV pill (kept) on the upper strip, RV total on the right of the RV bar Just RV (drop CV) CV only, no RV total
Q7 · roll dot fidelity How granular is the rolls indicator?

Akasha shows • / •• / ••• / •••• — discrete roll count. We could go further with roll quality (each dot tinted by that roll's value vs max).

Count only (4 dots, filled or dim) — matches akasha Count + quality tint per dot
Q8 · what to do when there's no rank yet Build never uploaded to leaderboard.

Rank module is the new headline. What does it say when the user hasn't submitted, or when their build doesn't qualify for any board?

"Not ranked — submit to a board" CTA in the same surface Hide module entirely Show CV-only score in the same chrome
